# AMA: Inside the OWASP State of Agentic AI Security & Governance

- **Channel:** Zenity
- **URL:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jK1Z7Z6zlW0

## Overview
A webinar presenting the second version of the OASP State of Agentic AI report, which documents the evolution of agentic AI security threats, governance frameworks, and industry trends over the past year. The panel discusses how forecasted threats from the first report have now become documented incidents with CVEs, and introduces new frameworks including an updated taxonomy, maturity model, and regulatory landscape analysis.

## What was covered
- Evolution from first to second report: shift from forecasting threats to documenting real incidents, CVEs, and vendor advisories across nearly all threat categories
- Updated agent taxonomy with new dimensions including agent types, domains, build methods, composition models, and autonomy levels (supervised, semi-autonomous, fully autonomous) to help organizations evaluate risk
- Threat analysis emphasizing prompt injection as a delivery mechanism rather than standalone vulnerability, and supply chain attacks as the fastest-moving attack surface targeting tools, memory, and dependencies
- Regulatory landscape changes including EU AI Act Article 72 drift detection requirements, incident reporting timelines (DORA 4 hours, NIS2 24 hours), and EU strict liability product liability framework effective December
- Introduction of AI SBOM (Software Bill of Materials) requirements adapted for agentic systems that assemble dependencies at runtime, extending beyond traditional build-time inventory
- Enterprise adoption maturity model mapping organizational adoption tiers (shadow AI, platforms, citizen developers, in-house agents, multi-agent systems) to appropriate governance levels
- Protocol landscape updates including Model Context Protocol (MCP) rapid adoption, supply chain security challenges, and the Postmark MCP incident demonstrating supply chain vulnerabilities
- Actionable guidance for organizations to discover and govern shadow AI, establish continuous oversight capabilities, and plan for strictest applicable regulatory timelines
